Intro

The Colombian student visa is a Visitor type visa that allows the foreigner to stay, live and study in the country at an officially recognized institution or program for up to 2 years.

Passport holders from any of the short-term visa exempted countries or territories normally can study and take lessons or courses in Colombia as long as their stay in the country doesn‚Äôt exceed 180 calendar days, they can fully enjoy their educational experience without worrying about visa formalities.

If the foreigner comes from a different country, or if the program or course has a length greater than 6 months, the foreigner must apply for this visa to comply with the migratory rules. Also, consider that every school, university or institution may have their own rules for accepting students and are independent from the migratory rules.

If you are planning to apply for this visa category, you must make sure that the course or program you are enrolling to is officially recognized by the Colombian government at any educational level and that they will issue the necessary documents and evidence to attach for your visa application. The Colombian education system levels include public and private institutions for elementary school, high school, University exchange programs, masters and doctorates.

Also, some other programs and courses are recognized by the Colombian government such as language learning institutions, among others.

It is important to note that the student visa is intended for individuals who will be studying¬†at least ten (10) hours per week during the whole duration of the program¬†and not for those who wish to travel to Colombia as tourists and take occasional classes.

üí° When a school or University is supporting a visa application for a foreign student, they must comply certain migratory rules, specially, they must report the student‚Äôs enrollment and assistance to the classes. In that sense, if the student does not show up to classes, the institution must report this to the authorities, causing the cancellation of the visa.

For second and further visas applications (even if they are not student visas), you will need to show evidence that you have successfully finished the studies program.

This visa category belongs to the visitor type (V), meaning that it doesn‚Äôt allow the foreigner to accrue time towards residency.

Unless the program allow virtual or at-distance education, if the foreigner stays out of the country for more than 90 days it will cause the automatic termination of the visa.

What are the benefits and restrictions for this visa?

Validity depends on the length of the program

This visa allows you to stay and study for up to two (2) years, depending on the duration of the program.

No beneficiaries except for post-graduate students

This visa does not allow applications for beneficiaries, except for postgraduate students.

Work permit only for post-graduate students

This type of visa does not allow you to work in Colombia for Colombian companies, since it doesn‚Äôt have a work permit, unless you are enrolled in a postgraduate program, such as a Master, a Doctorate or specialization, in which case you will be allowed to work up to twenty (20) hours per week, provided that the employer reports such circumstance to Migraci√≥n Colombia.

No time accrue towards residency

Since it is a visitor-type (V) visa, this won‚Äôt allow you to add time in order to apply for a residency or (R) visa.

How to get the student visa

As with any visa application, the process is done online, through the Ministry for Foreign Relationship‚Äôs website. on this form, other than providing your personal, professional and family information and data, you will be required to attach some documents that are described here below.

In order to apply for this visa, you will need to show evidence that you have been accepted on a studies program and that you have enough financial solvency to live in the country.

The specific requirements for a student visa may vary depending on your sponsorship. If you are applying with the sponsorship of a Colombian host, you will need to provide documentation of your sponsor‚Äôs financial solvency and the sponsor‚Äôs motivational letter. If you are applying with the sponsorship of an educational institution, they will present the application on your behalf. If you are applying on your own behalf, you will need to provide documentation of your own financial solvency and a motivational letter.

On your own behalf

Motivational letter

You will need to write a motivational letter informing your full identification (full legal name, passport number, birthdate, nationality and residency), explaining thoroughly and accurately the activities that you are planning to do under the student visa, including the name of the school, the address of the school, the type and name of the program/course you will attending, its schedule, the place where you will be staying and mention and give details from some contacts in the school.

Proof of your financial solvency

This can be accomplished by providing your bank statements from the previous six months, showing a monthly income of at least 10 Colombian minimum wages.

(As of 2023, the minimum wage in Colombia is $1.160.000 per month)

Sponsored by a Colombian host

You can get your visa application sponsored or backed up by a Colombian national or Colombian resident (a foreigner with a current valid Resident visa), if this is the case, you will need:

Letter from your sponsor

On this letter, your sponsor confirms that he/she will take full responsibility for your personal and financial well-being during your stay in Colombia, including covering all expenses related to transportation, accommodation and repatriation.

The sponsor needs to include his/her phone number, physical address, email address, the type of relationship with you (relative, fiance, friend, business partner, etc.), the manual signature (on pen) and a fingerprint (one fingerprint) of either the right or left index finger, or right or left thumb finger.

The sponsor also commits on this letter to inform the Colombian government if you stay in the country illegally and to ensure your return to your country of origin. Non-compliance with this rule may result in sanctions.‚Äç The sponsor also needs to state in this letter previously sponsored visa applications.

Additional Requirements

1. Admission certificate

Certificate of admission or enrollment in the educational institution indicating the degree or program for which the student is admitted or enrolled and its duration.

2. The entrance stamp, its extension, previous visa or salvoconducto

If you are applying from Colombia, you must show evidence of your current legal status in the country.

In most cases, this requirement is met by attaching your last Tourism Permit (Entrance Stamp), provided that it won‚Äôt expire during the 30 days of the visa processing. If you have extended your stay, you must submit your Tourism Permit Extension as well.

In other cases, you may already have a previous visa such as a student visa, if that is the case, you must attach it.

In some other cases, if you have overstayed or your current visa is about to expire, you must go to Migraci√≥n Colombia to get a Salvoconducto for a visa application, and attach it. Remember that there are two types of Salvoconducto: 1. To leave the country and 2. To apply for a visa. The one that you need is the last one.

If you are applying from your home country, you don‚Äôt need to attach any of the mentioned documents.

If you are applying from a third country, that is, not Colombia and not your country, you need to attach your residency visa of that third country.
