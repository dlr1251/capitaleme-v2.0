What are the benefits and restrictions for this visa?

Up to three years validity

The Colombian Investment Visa allows you to live in Colombia for up to three (3) years. It also allows you to get a foreign ID card (C√©dula de extranjer√≠a) and to affiliate to the Colombian public health system (EPS).

This visa admits beneficiaries

With this visa you will be able to bring your family with you as beneficiaries. That is, you can bring your spouse and/or children to live with you, once the main visa is approved.

Restricted work permit

This visa will have a work permit exclusively applicable to the company of which the person is a partner, shareholder, or owner.

Accrue time towards residency

Since it is a Migrant-type (M) visa, this visa allows you to add time in order to apply for a residency or (R) visa.

You will need to accrue five (5) consecutive years holding this visa to apply for the Colombian residency.

You can lose your visa if you stay out of the country for 6 continous months.

Specific Requirements

In order to apply for this visa, you will need to show evidence of your investment in a Colombian Company:

1. Request Letter

Letter of request in which the trade name, address, and Tax Identification Number (NIT) of the commercial company established or invested in are provided, along with the company‚Äôs main activity and the number of direct jobs it generates.

2. Proof of participation

Proof of the incorporation, participation, acquisition in the paid-up capital of the commercial company, or registration of foreign investment for an amount not less than one hundred (100) times the current legal monthly minimum wage (SMMLV) at the date of the application, and provide bank statements.

3. Share composition certificate

In the case of joint-stock companies, such as the SAS, the participation in the company will be accredited with a certificate of share composition, signed by a public accountant, indicating the amount of capital or registered and paid assets owned by the foreign applicant of the visa, which may not be less than the equivalent of one hundred (100) times the current legal monthly minimum wage (SMLMV) at the date of the application.
